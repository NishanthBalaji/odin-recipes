Parotta

Parotta is a popular South Indian flatbread made from maida (refined flour), which is layered and then rolled out before being shallow fried. It is a staple dish in Tamil Nadu and Kerala, often served with side dishes like chicken curry, mutton curry, or vegetable kurma.

The flaky and crispy texture of parotta makes it a favorite street food and a beloved accompaniment to various curries and gravies.

Ingredients

  • All-purpose flour (maida)
  • Water
  • Salt
  • Oil or ghee for frying

Steps

  1. Prepare the Dough: In a mixing bowl, combine all-purpose flour (maida) with water and salt to form a smooth and pliable dough. Let it rest for about 30 minutes.
  2. Divide and Roll: Divide the dough into small balls. Take one ball and roll it out into a thin circle. Apply oil or ghee on the surface and fold it into pleats. Roll the pleated dough into a spiral and tuck the end underneath.
  3. Flatten and Fry: Take the rolled dough and flatten it gently with your palm. Heat a skillet or griddle and place the flattened dough on it. Cook on medium heat, flipping occasionally, until both sides are golden brown and crispy.
  4. Serve Hot: Remove the parotta from the skillet and serve hot with your favorite side dish or curry.
