Mutton Biryani

Mutton Biryani is a fragrant and flavorful rice dish made with tender pieces of mutton, aromatic spices, and basmati rice. The mutton is marinated and cooked to perfection, infusing the rice with rich flavors and creating a satisfying and hearty meal.

This traditional dish is often garnished with fried onions, boiled eggs, and fresh coriander, making it a complete and delicious meal that is perfect for special occasions or a luxurious treat.

Ingredients

  • Basmati rice
  • Boneless mutton pieces
  • Yogurt
  • Onions
  • Tomatoes
  • Ginger-garlic paste
  • Spices (e.g., garam masala, turmeric, red chili powder)
  • Fresh coriander and mint leaves
  • Saffron soaked in milk

Steps

  1. Marinate the Mutton: In a bowl, combine mutton pieces with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, and spices. Let it marinate for at least an hour.
  2. Cook the Rice: In a large pot, parboil the basmati rice with whole spices like cardamom, cinnamon, and cloves. Drain and set aside.
  3. Cook the Mutton: In a large skillet, heat oil and fry onions until golden brown. Add tomatoes and the marinated mutton, cooking until the mutton is tender.
  4. Layer the Biryani: In a heavy-bottomed pot, layer the cooked mutton and parboiled rice. Top with saffron milk, fried onions, and fresh coriander and mint leaves.
  5. Dum Cooking: Seal the pot with a tight-fitting lid or dough, and cook on low heat for about 20-30 minutes to let the flavors meld together. Serve hot.
